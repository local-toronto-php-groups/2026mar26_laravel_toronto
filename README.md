##
After one of the snowiest winters, it is time to have a spring meet-up.

Your fellow Toronto Artisans are curious about the new Laravel AI package.

So, we are going to do a hackathon-ish workshop. Or, a workshop-ish hackathon, to get our hands on it. 

The Laravel AI package was published as beta, on Feb 05th. It might be GA released by the 26th, or maybe not. It is a new package. 

These days, each developer has their own custom local development environment. Dev environments are more intense than ever. To the extent that if we go to the meet-up with incomplete local dev set-ups on our laptops, we could spend the entire meet-up on its completion. To keep the focus on AI apps, please complete your local set-up prior. 

You also need API access to your favourite LLM(s). Google offers free access to developers, for prototyping. No CC required. 

I am personally in the midst of developing an AI app for my podcasts. However, I am building my own custom functionality, in lieu of installing the new AI package. 

A very special thank you to those who are active on our Discord server -- it is a big help for me to hear from you outside of the meet-ups.

Thank you to Maple for hosting, and sponsoring the pizza.

Schedule:
- 6:00pm to 7:00pm: meet-and-greet
- 7:00pm to 9:00pm: presentation

-Bob

##
<h2>Special Notes</h2>
For this meet-up, there will be no presentations. We will have AI presentations at our April 23rd meet-up at Vetster.

So, yes, bring your laptops. 

There are so many ways developers are approaching their development environment, so it is best that you have your dev environment ready-to-go at the meet-up. Otherwise, there is a very real chance, as has happened in the past, that we spend the entire meet-up on setting up dev environments. Things used to be so straight forward!

I assume that everyone is using AI in their software development. However, it does not matter how you prefer to do AI in your software development for this meet-up. It will be interesting how everyone approaches it. 

Personally, I am still using Claude in the browser -- with the "Pro" plan. You can do code generation in the browser for free, for Claude, Gemini, etc. In my experience, each is a different experience. For the purposes of our hackaton-ish meet-up, Generating code in the browser without a paid subscription is just fine. There is no pre-requisite of having a paid plan. 

I am interested in seeing someone driving Claude Code at the command line. I may not be at my computer much!

It is important to have a API access to at least one model for the meet-up. Gemini is, to the best of my knowledge, the only model offering API access with a generous free tier, no CC required.

Personally, I am currently building an application for my PHP Serverless News podcast. To look at, among other things, RSS feeds automatically, have Gemini produce a summary, and upload the results as a web page, on a web server. I may or may not, by the time of the meet-up, have my app send to a WordPress site via the WP REST API. WP7 pre-release is a week before our meet-up, so we'll see. If anyone is curious, I can put the repo on the screen, or go through it on my laptop. 

The overhead will be available for impromptu lightening talks, explanations, speeches... I confess that I will be asking about Laravel's Boost. Claude told me that I can use Boost only at the CLI. So, yeah... that is on my "to do" list at the meet-up. Let me know if you are in the mood to expound on this.

BTW, I am running FrankenPHP on my local Docker container. It is working well. However, Forge does not set up FrankenPHP by default, so I will have some work to do when my app is in prod.
